01 The moment

Additive manufacturing is becoming ordinary infrastructure.

Schools, labs, manufacturers, marketplaces, makerspaces, and homes are gaining extraordinary new creative capacity. That same accessibility creates a public-safety gap: digital designs for firearms and regulated components can be fabricated outside some traditional manufacturing and distribution checkpoints.

State policy is beginning to move toward performance-based safeguards. Implementers need a standard that reduces risk, withstands public scrutiny, and protects lawful making.

03 The system

A fast first review layer for additive-manufacturing workflows.

PrintGuard combines known-file evidence with shape-aware classification, producing a screening result organizations can inspect and document.

  1. 01

    Accept

    Screen supported mesh files during publication, intake, or pre-print review.

  2. 02

    Understand

    Extract mesh statistics, point-cloud, voxel, and multiview features.

  3. 03

    Compare

    Check exact SHA-256 evidence, near-geometry matches, and classifier scores.

  4. 04

    Route

    Return a review-oriented decision with supporting scores, then log the result locally.

07 The opportunity

Help define what good looks like.

PrintGuard is seeking pilot and technical partners to test a practical reference implementation for emerging performance standards.

California AB 2047 proposes performance guidance, a protected reference-file library, and regular update requirements. New York enacted a blocking-technology framework whose implementation depends on technical feasibility, a working group, and rulemaking.

The durable answer will require manufacturers, researchers, public institutions, and policymakers to build it together.
